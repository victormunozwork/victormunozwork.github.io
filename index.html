The kind of problems I work on

Practical help for the parts that became difficult to manage.

I usually join teams that already have tools and processes in place. My role is to understand what is happening, find the gaps and create a clearer way forward.

01

“We have a process, but it is difficult to follow.”

I map what is currently happening and turn it into a workflow, checklist, SOP or template the team can return to.

02

“We have several tools, but they do not feel connected.”

I learn how each platform is being used, identify the missing steps and create a clearer way to move information between them.

03

“There are too many small operational things to remember.”

I help manage recurring tasks, registrations, follow-ups, participant journeys, files and internal tracking.

04

“The report says one thing, but the source says another.”

I investigate the data, trace where the difference started and help repair the process behind the report.

How I work

I am comfortable going inside a system and learning how it really works.

Over time, I found that the work I enjoy most happens between people and tools.

It is not only setting up the platform. It is understanding who needs to use it, what they need to know and what could go wrong after everyone returns to their normal work.

I am calm, curious and comfortable working independently. Give me the goal, access to the current process and the people I need to ask. I will explore how things work, document what I find and keep the work moving.

My background includes five years in cybersecurity in banking, followed by data engineering, reporting and digital operations. It is a slightly unusual path, but it has made me careful with systems and very comfortable with messy details.

My job is often to notice the gaps, then make them easier to manage.

My approach

Understand first. Organize second.

1. Follow the current process I learn what the team is doing today, not what the documentation says should happen.
2. Find the gaps I look for unclear ownership, repeated manual work and information that gets lost between tools.
3. Build a practical structure I create workflows, documentation, trackers or small automations that fit the real team.
4. Make it easier to continue The goal is a process people can understand, use and maintain after the initial setup.
